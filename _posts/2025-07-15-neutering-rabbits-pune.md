Neutering refers to the surgical removal of reproductive organs — castration for males (bucks) and spaying for females (does). For rabbits, it is one of the most important health decisions you can make as a pet owner. At **Shivraj Pet Clinic & Lab**, we have safely performed rabbit neutering for pet parents across Pimple Nilakh, Baner, Aundh, and Wakad.

## Why Should You Neuter Your Rabbit?

**Prevents reproductive cancers** — Uterine cancer affects over 80% of unspayed female rabbits by age 5. Spaying completely eliminates this risk.

**Reduces aggression and territorial behaviour** — Unneutered rabbits often bite, lunge, grunt, and spray urine to mark territory. Neutering dramatically reduces these behaviours in both males and females.

**Allows safe bonding between rabbits** — A neutered male and spayed female can live together harmoniously without risk of pregnancy.

**Improves hygiene** — Neutered rabbits spray urine far less, making their living space cleaner and easier to maintain.

**Longer, healthier life** — Studies consistently show neutered rabbits live significantly longer than intact ones. Removal of reproductive organs eliminates associated diseases.

## Common Myths About Rabbit Neutering

**"They are too small for surgery"** — Not true. We have safely neutered rabbits weighing as little as 1.2 kg. The key is appropriate anaesthesia and surgical technique, not size.

**"It changes their personality"** — Neutering actually makes rabbits calmer and more affectionate. Their core personality remains intact.

**"Rabbits recover too slowly"** — Rabbits typically return to normal activity within 48–72 hours when proper pain management and post-operative care protocols are followed.

## When Is the Right Age?

Males can be castrated from 3.5 to 4 months of age, once the testicles have fully descended. Females are ideally spayed between 5 and 6 months. Surgery in older rabbits carries slightly higher risk due to fatty tissue around the reproductive organs, but is still routinely performed with appropriate pre-operative screening.
