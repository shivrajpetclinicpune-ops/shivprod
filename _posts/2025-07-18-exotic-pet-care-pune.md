## Common Health Issues in Exotic Pets

Exotic pets often mask their discomfort. By the time signs appear, the issue can be serious. Here is what to watch for by species:

**Birds:** Egg binding, feather plucking, beak overgrowth. Signs include fluffed feathers, lethargy, and tail bobbing.

**Rabbits:** GI stasis, dental malocclusion, heat stroke. Watch for reduced appetite, drooling, and unusually quiet behaviour.

**Turtles:** Shell rot, Vitamin A deficiency. Signs include swollen eyes, soft shell, and nasal discharge.

**Hamsters:** Wet tail disease, overgrown teeth. Lethargy, diarrhoea, and weight loss are common indicators.
