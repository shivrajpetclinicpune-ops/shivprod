Over 80% of dogs and 70% of cats develop some form of dental disease by the age of 3 — yet dental health remains one of the most overlooked aspects of pet care. At **Shivraj Pet Clinic & Lab**, we perform professional dental scaling and oral health assessments to prevent pain, tooth loss, and the serious systemic diseases that dental infection can cause.

## Why Dental Health Matters

Dental disease is not just about bad breath. When bacteria accumulate in the mouth and form tartar (hardened calculus), they can enter the bloodstream and cause:

- **Heart valve disease** — bacteria settling on heart valves
- **Kidney damage** — chronic low-grade infection taxes kidney filtration
- **Liver disease** — bacterial toxins processed by the liver
- **Chronic pain** — loose, infected teeth cause constant discomfort that pets hide

Dogs and cats do not show pain the way humans do. A pet with severe dental disease often continues eating — right up until they cannot.

## Signs Your Pet Needs Dental Attention

- Bad breath (beyond normal "dog breath")
- Yellow or brown deposits on teeth near the gum line
- Red, swollen, or bleeding gums
- Loose teeth
- Drooling more than usual
- Pawing at the mouth
- Preference for soft food or dropping food while eating
- Facial swelling under the eye (often indicates a tooth root abscess)

## The Dental Scaling Procedure

Professional dental scaling at Shivraj Pet Clinic involves:

1. **Pre-anaesthetic bloodwork** — essential before general anaesthesia
2. **General anaesthesia** — pets must be fully anaesthetised for thorough and safe scaling. "Anaesthesia-free dentistry" is ineffective and stressful — a conscious pet cannot hold still for sub-gingival (below gum line) cleaning where disease lives.
3. **Scaling** — ultrasonic and hand instruments remove tartar from all tooth surfaces
4. **Probing** — checking for periodontal pockets and loose teeth
5. **Extraction** — severely diseased teeth are extracted rather than left to cause ongoing pain and infection
6. **Polishing** — smooths enamel to slow future tartar accumulation

## Maintaining Oral Health at Home

- **Tooth brushing** — daily brushing with a pet-safe toothpaste is the gold standard. Start slowly, let your pet lick the toothpaste first, introduce the brush gradually.
- **Dental chews** — those with the VOHC (Veterinary Oral Health Council) seal have evidence behind them
- **Dental diets** — certain prescription diets help reduce tartar mechanically
- **Water additives** — some products reduce bacterial counts in drinking water

Professional dental scaling is typically needed every 1–3 years depending on your pet's breed and oral hygiene routine. Small breeds (Shih Tzu, Dachshund, Pomeranians) tend to develop tartar faster and may need annual scaling.
