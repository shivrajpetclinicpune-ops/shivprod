Birds are prey animals with a powerful instinct to hide illness. In the wild, showing weakness attracts predators — so birds mask signs of disease until they can no longer compensate. By the time a bird looks obviously sick, it has often been ill for days. This is why bird owners must know the subtle early signs of illness, and why **acting within hours** is often the difference between recovery and death.

## Why Bird Illness is Deceptive

A bird that appears healthy in the morning can be critically ill by evening. Their small body mass means they have very little reserve — dehydration, hypoglycaemia, and respiratory failure can develop within hours once compensation fails. Never wait to see if a sick bird "gets better on its own."

## Early Signs Your Bird is Unwell

**Posture:**
- Sitting on the bottom of the cage rather than on perches
- Fluffed up feathers — the most recognisable sign of illness in birds
- Head tucked under the wing when not sleeping
- Leaning to one side or having difficulty balancing

**Eyes:**
- Partially closed or squinted eyes during the day
- Discharge around the eyes
- Sunken eyes indicate significant dehydration

**Breathing:**
- Tail bobbing with each breath — indicates respiratory distress
- Open-mouth breathing or clicking sounds
- Voice changes or loss of vocalisation in normally vocal birds

**Droppings:**
- Normal bird droppings have three components: dark green/brown faeces, white urates, and clear liquid urine
- Abnormal: all green (bile), blood-tinged, completely liquid, black/tarry, or significantly decreased in number

**Feeding and Behaviour:**
- Reduced appetite or interest in food
- Drinking significantly more or less water than usual
- Uncharacteristic quietness in normally active birds
- Loss of interest in toys, social interaction, or familiar activities

## Common Diseases in Pet Birds

**Avian polyomavirus** — affects young birds, sudden death or feather abnormalities.

**Psittacine beak and feather disease (PBFD)** — progressive feather destruction and beak abnormalities in parrots.

**Aspergillosis** — fungal respiratory infection, common in immunocompromised birds or those kept in damp environments.

**Egg binding** — female birds unable to pass an egg. Emergency requiring immediate veterinary care.

**Crop infection (candidiasis)** — yeast infection of the crop causing regurgitation and sour smell.

**Proventricular dilatation disease (PDD)** — progressive neurological and gastrointestinal disease in parrots.

## What to Do When Your Bird is Sick

1. **Keep your bird warm** — place the cage in a warm (28–30°C), quiet location. Heat support is critical as sick birds lose body temperature rapidly.
2. **Do not offer new foods or supplements** — stick to familiar foods that the bird is comfortable eating.
3. **Call and come immediately** — do not wait for the "next available appointment." A sick bird needs same-day care.
4. **Transport carefully** — minimise stress during transport. Cover most of the carrier to create a dark, calm environment.
