One of the most common causes of pet poisoning that we see at **Shivraj Pet Clinic & Lab** is well-intentioned owners giving human medicines to their pets. What is safe — or even therapeutic — in humans can be fatal in dogs and cats. **Never give your pet any human medication without first consulting your veterinarian.**

## Most Dangerous Human Medicines for Dogs and Cats

### 1. Paracetamol (Crocin, Dolo, Tylenol)

**Extremely toxic to cats. Dangerous to dogs in any dose.**

Cats completely lack the liver enzyme (UGT1A6) needed to metabolise paracetamol. Even a single tablet can cause fatal liver failure and haemoglobin destruction in cats. Dogs can also suffer severe liver damage. There is no safe dose — never give paracetamol to any pet.

Signs of paracetamol toxicity: facial swelling, brown/blue gums (methaemoglobinaemia), laboured breathing, jaundice, collapse.

### 2. Ibuprofen and Diclofenac (Brufen, Combiflam, Voveran)

**Toxic to both dogs and cats.**

NSAIDs like ibuprofen cause gastrointestinal ulceration, kidney failure, and neurological signs in pets. Even one tablet of ibuprofen can cause severe gastric ulcers and kidney damage in a medium-sized dog. Diclofenac has caused mass deaths in vultures in India and is similarly dangerous to companion animals.

### 3. Aspirin

Aspirin is occasionally used in dogs at very specific doses under veterinary supervision — but even here, errors can be fatal. Cats cannot metabolise aspirin at all. A single aspirin tablet every 48 hours can accumulate to toxic levels in cats. Never use aspirin without explicit veterinary direction and dosing.

### 4. Antidepressants and Sleep Aids

Human antidepressants (SSRIs, tricyclics) and sedatives cause serious neurological toxicity in pets — tremors, seizures, cardiac arrhythmias, and coma. These are among the most common causes of accidental pet poisoning.

### 5. Xylitol (in sugar-free products and some medicines)

Xylitol is a sugar substitute found in sugar-free gum, some peanut butter brands, vitamin gummies, and oral care products. In dogs, it causes a rapid, severe drop in blood sugar (hypoglycaemia) and liver failure. Even small amounts — the equivalent of a few pieces of sugar-free gum — can be fatal to a small dog.

### 6. Vitamins and Supplements

**Vitamin D (cholecalciferol)** in excess is a common rat poison ingredient and causes fatal hypercalcaemia in pets. **Vitamin C** in high doses causes gastrointestinal upset. **Iron supplements** cause gastrointestinal haemorrhage and liver damage.

## What to Do in Case of Accidental Ingestion

If your pet has consumed any human medication:
1. Do not wait for symptoms to appear
2. Call us immediately at **077569 65169**
3. Note the exact medication name, dose/tablet strength, and approximately how much was consumed
4. Bring the packaging with you

Early treatment — within the first 1–2 hours — dramatically improves outcomes for most toxicities. Do not induce vomiting at home without veterinary guidance, as this can sometimes cause additional harm.
