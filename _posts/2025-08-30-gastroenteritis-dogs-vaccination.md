Vomiting and diarrhoea are among the most common reasons dog owners visit **Shivraj Pet Clinic & Lab**. Gastroenteritis — inflammation of the gastrointestinal tract — can range from a 24-hour dietary upset to a life-threatening infection requiring hospitalisation. Knowing when to act quickly can save your dog's life.

## Common Causes of Gastroenteritis in Dogs

**Dietary indiscretion** — eating spoiled food, garbage, or something from outside. The most common cause. Usually self-limiting within 24–48 hours.

**Viral infections:**
- **Parvovirus (Parvo)** — bloody, foul-smelling diarrhoea with severe vomiting and rapid deterioration. Almost entirely preventable with vaccination. Mortality in unvaccinated puppies can exceed 80%.
- **Distemper** — causes gastroenteritis along with respiratory and neurological signs.
- **Coronavirus** — milder gastrointestinal disease.

**Bacterial infections** — Salmonella, Campylobacter, Clostridium — often from contaminated food or water.

**Parasites** — roundworms, giardia, and coccidia commonly cause diarrhoea, particularly in puppies.

**Pancreatitis** — inflammation of the pancreas from fatty food consumption. Very common in Indian dogs fed table scraps.

**Intussusception** — telescoping of the intestine into itself. An emergency requiring immediate surgery.

## When to Come to the Clinic Immediately

Do not wait if your dog shows:
- Blood in the vomit or stool
- Projectile vomiting or vomiting more than 3 times in an hour
- Bloated or painful abdomen
- Extreme lethargy or collapse
- Complete refusal to drink water
- Signs of dehydration — dry gums, skin that does not spring back when pinched
- A puppy with any vomiting and diarrhoea — puppies dehydrate fast

**A vomiting and diarrhoea puppy that has not completed its vaccination series is a Parvo emergency until proven otherwise.** Come immediately.

## Treatment

Treatment depends on the underlying cause and severity. Mild cases may need only fluid support and a bland diet. Severe cases require:
- IV fluid therapy for dehydration correction
- Anti-nausea medication
- Gastrointestinal protectants
- Antibiotics when bacterial infection is confirmed
- Deworming if parasites are identified
- Hospitalisation for monitoring and intensive care in critical cases

## The Role of Vaccination

**Parvovirus vaccination is the single most important thing you can do to protect your dog from life-threatening gastroenteritis.** The DHPPiL vaccine (given at 2–2.5 months and annually thereafter) provides excellent protection against Parvovirus, Distemper, and Coronavirus.

Unvaccinated dogs can contract Parvo from contaminated soil — the virus survives in the environment for months. Even dogs that never leave the house are not fully protected without vaccination.
